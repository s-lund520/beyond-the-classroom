Employment

Finding meaningful work is an important part of building independence and confidence. These resources offer job placement, on-the-job training, and long-term vocational support for students and young adults in Vermont.

HireAbility Vermont

Vermont's official vocational rehabilitation program — where ability meets opportunity. Offers employment services for youth and adults, including job placement, on-the-job support, and resources for parents, schools, and community partners. A great first stop for students transitioning to work.

Visit site

APSE — Association of People Supporting Employment First

The only national organization focused exclusively on inclusive employment and career advancement for people with disabilities. Promotes the Employment First movement — the belief that real jobs for real wages should be the first and preferred option for individuals with disabilities. A great resource for families, advocates, and employers.

🌟 Success Story

A U-32 student shares their journey

My name is Shea. I am 21 years old, born and raised in Central Vermont. I attended U-32 Middle and High School and graduated with my class in 2023. I am furthering my education by attending the U-32 Transition Academy. In my free time, I enjoy reading fantasy fiction, spending time with my pets, and attending karate on Tuesday and Wednesday afternoons.

In the Transition program, I learned many useful lifelong skills — from cooking on a stove and following recipes to working in the school library, checking books in, dusting shelves, and organizing in alphabetical order. That last part took time, but I got the hang of it.

In 2023, I had the opportunity to learn job skills out in the community. I started volunteering at Central Vermont Medical Center in the cafe — wiping tables, filling napkin holders, labeling bags, and making sure everyone had a smile on their face. My job coach, Jill Moser, has been a huge part of my growth. Though it took time to build our relationship, she believed in me and pushed me to keep going even when it seemed impossible.

At the start of 2026, I was offered a per-diem position in Nutrition and Food Services at Central Vermont Medical Center — where I still receive support from my job coach as I learn the ins and outs of my new role.

"I feel very blessed to have the ability to work in such a great environment. I remember thinking that a job was never something I'd be able to do. I always thought I'd be at home, wondering what it would be like to have a job while watching everyone around me work. But today I am here to say I made it! I have a job that sometimes kicks my butt, lol. But I wouldn't want it any other way."

Having a huge support circle at work has made learning so much easier. My job gives me the ability to socialize, meet new people, stay busy, and make my own money. I love that my workplace is understanding and flexible.

When it comes to everything I have accomplished, I am most proud of all the progress I have made. When I started, I struggled to communicate my needs and complete basic tasks without becoming distracted. Today, I can proudly share that I am able to communicate my needs, and I have worked very hard to complete tasks independently without getting overwhelmed.

— Shea, U-32 Graduate & Transition Academy Student
